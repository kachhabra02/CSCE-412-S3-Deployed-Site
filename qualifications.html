Areas of Interest

Since beginning my time at Texas A&M, my main two areas of interest in computer science have been software engineering and data science/analytics. As I have experienced more of what each has to offer, I am not really interested in the full breadth of software engineering but rather the subset of back-end development. I am still interested in data science/analytics as the ability to derive useful, actionable insights from raw data greatly interests and fascinates me.

While doing an internship at Phillips 66, I was exposed to two more career paths which I would be interested in exploring: data engineering and business analyst. Designing/maintaining databases and engineering data pipelines seems both interesting and very useful to all companies. A business analyst lies on the border of the business and IT, taking the needs of the business and the capabilities of IT to architect and lead projects which drive value in key business areas.
